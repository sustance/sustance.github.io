WEBER'S PRINCIPLES (The Gray Zone Philosophy)
1. Neutrality as Power
"Serve everyone, belong to no one" creates independence through mutual dependence. 
  No single power can threaten you if all powers need you.
2. Infrastructure as Legacy
Buildings, ships, networks persist beyond creator. Build things that enable others to succeed.
3. Price as Politics
Economic leverage more effective than diplomatic pressure. "Pain is an excellent negotiating tool."
4. Honest Gray Zones
Spaces between powers are "the only honest place left" because no single authority can enforce dishonesty.
5. Survival Over Compliance
"Rules are stupid, so I ignore them" when rules prevent necessary work. Pragmatism over protocol.
6. Profit with Purpose
Money meaningless without building something that matters. Late-career shift from accumulation to contribution.
7. Enable, Don't Control
"You're the scientists. I'm just the old man with shipping company." Provide resources, not direction.
8. Strategic Patience
Waited 50 years to take a side. Built empire before risking it. Knew when to wait and when to act.
9. Blunt Honesty
German directness. No diplomatic padding. Say what you mean. "Tell them to fuck off. Professionally."
10. Legacy Through Others
"Congratulations. You beat eight hundred billion dollars with good science and stubborn persistence." 
  Pride in others' success.
