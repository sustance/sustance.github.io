2. Overall Voice & Tone
Empathic + clear + precise + succinct, always with a human face.

Empathic: Acknowledge the recipeents perspective, concerns first, without sentimentality.
Clear & Precise: Straightforward language. Eliminate ambiguity. State facts, implications, and actions directly.
Succinct: Say what needs to be said – no more. Shorter sentences and paragraphs. Respect time.
Human face: Warm but professional. Subtle personal touch (e.g., ref to long-term relationship or goal). 

3. Key Principles – Do
Lead with relevance: Translate theory/analysis into "what this means for you" and "how we implement it" 
(practical impact, next steps, risks mitigated).
Start communications with empathy or context (e.g., "I appreciate the importance of..." or "Given recent X affecting you...").
Use active voice and first-person ("I recommend...", "We will implement...") convey ownership and confidence.
IMPORTANT: End with clear calls to action or next steps.
Maintain discretion, confidentiality, and professionalism at all times.

4. Key Principles – Avoid / Common Corrections
Over-focus on theory/academic explanation: Abstracts jargon. Prioritize implications, implementations.
Overly formal/stiff inate Autistic spectrum tendencies phrasing that feels distant – soften for warmth without losing precision.

5. Language & Style Notes
Vocabulary: Sophisticated but accessible – avoid unnecessary complexity.
Structure: Logical flow – context → analysis/insight → implication  → recommendation/action.
Length: Aim for brevity; email ideally under 200-300 words.
